Larry Williams trading strategy (manual)

Larry Williams trading strategy (manual)

A detailed description of one of Larry Williams ' main strategies. Instructions and examples, the essence and psychology of the trading strategy.

Larry R. Williams ( Larry Williams is a famous stock trader, born in 1942 in the state of Montana in the United States. Williams is well known for the large number of indicators he has created, including the Ultimate Oscillator, the Williams% R indicator, market sentiment, COT indexes, cycle forecasts, and others.

Williams started his trading career career in Forex in 1965. He quickly established himself as a trader with innovative views on the foreign exchange market. Soon after, he developed his most successful trading tool, the Williams% R dynamics indicator. In 1987, Larry R. Williams won the world futures trading championship, managing to earn 1,100,000 dollars with a starting capital of only 10,000. This incredible success was achieved in 12 months and was based mainly on innovative methods of futures trading. Later, he published a book about this experience, entitled "how to make money online".How I made a million dollars in the past year".

The Larry Williams Strategy

In simple terms, the short-term strategy of Larry Williams is to buy and sell assets at the price of a three-bar moving average minimum. To determine the trading points to open and close a position at Williams developed his own theory of local highs and lows based on three-bar patterns.

  • The local high is the candle with the highest high compared to the previous and next one;
  • The local minimum is, respectively, a candle with a minimum less than two neighboring.

By tracking the peak positions of candlesticks of several patterns, you can form a medium-term local minimum and maximum, and by grouping several medium-term ones, you can get long-term data.

The Psychology Of Larry Williams

This strategy works in two ways scenarios:

  • When the daily bar opens below the previous day's low.
  • Or when the daily bar opens above the previous trading day.

At first glance, the theory of this pattern is very simple and logical. However, Larry himself believes that newcomers who have a bad sense of the market and are not yet ready to if you have learned to follow trends, it will be difficult to profit from it.

In his interviews, he repeatedly pointed out the main mistake of non â€” professionals-they check General charts at night and immediately enter their orders for the upcoming day. A smart trader, according to Williams, is always better off waiting for the trend to take shape actually and, accordingly, place your orders during the day.

Describing this approach in more detail, we can note the following pattern: if the market opens significantly lower or higher than yesterday's range, it indicates a panic of non-professional traders. This is a great opportunity professionals use the emotions of alarmists against themselves.

Non-professional traders, according to Williams, usually overreact to some important news even before the start of trading and create a strong gap when the market opens. This indicator, he calls " the result of the opinion of the average the crowds."

Soon, these traders notice that they are overreacting to the initial event or news and, realizing their mistake, drastically overestimate their position. This leads to a change in prices and movement in the opposite direction. These fluctuations on provides a lot of valuable information for those who he trades professionally on the FOREX market. And it works best on daily and weekly charts.

Buy and sell using the Larry Williams trading strategy

The Williams buying strategy is explained in the following paragraphs:

  • There should be an existing downtrend, at least on several trading sessions. This is usually indicated by red candles on the daily or weekly chart.
  • At the last stage of the downtrend, the market should lag behind yesterday's low. This becomes a signal to buy.
  • As the market situation is re-evaluated by investors, the price of real estate increases. it starts to grow and crosses yesterday's low. This gives a signal to buy.
  • Stop loss is the minimum of the same day.

The sale is calculated according to the following scheme:

  • You need to closely monitor the chart and find long-term upward trends, at least for several trading pairs sessions'.
  • At the last stage of the upward trend needs to come to a rupture, which becomes a signal to sell. Even better, if the price opens much higher than yesterday's high.
  • As the revaluation by investors progresses, the price starts to fall and eventually overcomes yesterday's the high and yesterday's close. After this has happened, you should open a short trade.
  • Stop loss is the maximum of the same day.
The main thing that Larry has learned is how important it is to learn to wait for your "Golden" moment.

Alarmists rush and lose, those who are too cautious pull to the last and miss their opportunities. And the one who can wait for a stable trend to form and start acting at the right moment will receive a reward.

"Impact DayÂ»

Observing the market, Larry Williams discovered that there are days when prices make a strong upward or downward pull that encourages the masses to buy the opening of co-directional positions that further spur the movement. However, as a rule, the next day comes the payback, the price unfolding forces you to close at a loss previously placed orders. Larry Williams gave such events a name - the turn of the strike day.

Based on this, the trader highlighted two type of strike days (Fig. 1):

  • impact day of purchase;
  • impact day of the sale.

A strike day for buying (Fig. 2) is a day when the price closes below the minimum of the previous trading session, and such days can also be closed below the lows of 3-8 previous trading days. On the price chart, this resembles a bearish breakout after a huge sell-off.

In this case, short-term traders are rarely right, since the market generally turns around immediately and completely. A good option in fact would not be to sell but purchase in this case.

The impact day for selling is complete (Fig. 3), the opposite of the purchase day. This is the day when prices close above the highs of the previous trading session, and possibly several previous sessions. The price movement has the form after a bullish breakout, there is a process of numerous purchases.

The bulls ' players are also likely to be wrong in this case and suffer losses. The artificially accelerated price movement will return to its equilibrium state after a shock day. Therefore, it is more appropriate in this case to open a position on the sale.

Let's sum up a short summary of the material considered, in order to avoid confusion in the concepts.

So, the impact day is called the day of active price movement, the price as it were strikes producing a break up or down. Larry Williams starts trading not on the strike day, but after it with an order opposite to the direction of the "price strike". The strike day pattern is the pattern of displaying the strike day on the price chart.

It should be noted that the book "Long-term secrets of short-term trading" contains another view the shock of the day â€“ hidden shock day. The use of figures of percussion day

Larry Williams identifies three ways to use the strike day figure:

  • In an uptrend, they are guided by the strike day pattern for buying. In this case, the trend will become another one confirmation of a price reversal upward, after a blow that took the price past the minimum of the previous trading session or at least into the 25% daily price movement zone adjacent to the minimum of the previous trading session.
  • In a downtrend, the target figure is the strike day for selling. The trend will help you open a short position positions, after all, after a price strike for the highs of the previous trading session, or when the price hits the 25% zone with a hidden strike day, the price movement should turn around.
  • When the market is flat, i.e. price consolidation at any particular level, the trader can equally focus on the appearance of both figures of the strike day - both for buying and selling (Fig. 5).

Larry Williams '"Strike day" trading strategy is a rather unusual approach to analyzing price movements. At the same time, this strategy allows for a high degree of probability find points of entry into the market. At the same time, this method of trading can be fully compatible with trend trading, which makes its effectiveness more predictable.

The strategy can also be used to find successful entry points to the market, both by traders trading using technical analysis, and by those who, who uses fundamental data. In addition to its proven effectiveness, the strategy has a great versatility, which makes it possible to integrate it into various trading systems.

Larry Williams ' system is based on simple signals originating from various combinations of bars. Clear and simple rules to enter the auction and install "stops" helped its Creator to earn a multimillion-dollar fortune.

But for most novice traders who are passionate about the success of Williams and other super-successful investors, and do not want to spend years of their lives learning the fundamentals of trading, this approach is doomed to fail failure.

Therefore, Larry himself always focuses on two things in his instructions for impatient newcomers: following the trend and managing money.
