The growth of my dividend payments for first-class US equity was 8.1% in terms year-on-year. Regular organic dividend increases are one of the most important reasons that simply can't be avoided it is impossible to ignore when it comes to dividend investments.

Stocks can rise and fall in value. Ferocious corrections can rage, passing or not passing into crises. The most terrible forecasts can be made, and the most experienced ones can lose their teeth from volatility speculators'. But steadily dripping into the account and equally steadily growing dividends allow you to look at all this madness of fear or greed with a slight smile.

According to the latest reporting issue of the heading, the expected annual dividend is$ 13047 after taxes. An 8.1% increase in dollars means that if I if you owned the same amount of assets a year ago, you could expect annual dividends of only $ 11,992. This difference with the current dividend of$ 1055 is an organic increase in dividends that occurred on its own, without the need to add even a penny of your own money, reinvest even a cent, or move even a finger finger.

Money that grows on its own is cool. And the cash flow, which grows by itself - this is just fabulous!

Dividend growth works according to a simple, but very pleasant scheme: you just see in one day that the company has announced dividends that have become several times higher percentages (and sometimes - a couple of dozen percent) higher than they were in the previous quarter or year.

And here I can't help but cite a very real, life-like example. In the October 2019 issue of the "living on dividends" column, I announced that I had achieved financial independence. All my basic ones the needs for food, housing, clothing, and even investment for the son are covered by passive cash flow. I recorded the number of assets as of that date. The amount that brought me the desired level: $ 8,600 a year.

And now the dividend growth comes into play. If I own the same amount of assets, I can receive $ 8,979 in annual dividends already. This is an increase of$ 379 or 4.2%. It's only been 5 months, and that's the difference. Ask yourself: has your salary increased by this amount in less than six months? Have you had your salary increased by 4.2% in dollars since 2020? And is there any hope that it will be increased for you? And if you take into account the recent collapse of the ruble exchange rate, then your income in the in dollar terms, most likely, they have collapsed.

Dividends on shares grow by themselves. You don't need to ask your superiors for height. It goes absolutely organically - an absolutely passive increase in absolutely passive income!

Good luck in the future investments!
